Skills for Assertiveness – I statements

I
statements

“I” statements are among the most powerful you can make, both for yourself and others

In “I” statements you are affirming who you are and what you want

“I” statements can be used in a variety of ways-

  1. Situation
  2. Interpretation and understanding
  3. Feelings and Emotions
  4. Wants and Needs
  5. Future Actions

Using them is the hallmark of assertiveness

Examples:

  1. I have been asked to worked two hours extra every day
  2. I get the impression that you are interested in the product
  3. I am on time
